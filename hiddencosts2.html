Hidden Costs

The hidden costs of being a postgraduate researcher disproportionately impact certain demographics. In particular, international students, students with dependents and students with disabilities are most affected.

International Students

The average international student has to pay £168 more in rent every month than a home student, this is often due to the lack of a British guarantor. For the same reason, international students are often subject to much higher desposits for renting and are often asked to pay multiple installments of rent in advance.

After paying their rent, bills and food*, the average international student living in Bristol has just £236 left for everything else in the month. This includes travel, resources for study and any unexpected payments that they may have to make. Many students rely on savings, while others take out loans to survive during the cost of living crisis.

*Using the national average of £194 per month spent on food per individual - Survey conducted by Nimblefins 2023.

Outside of the monthly expenses, international studens are subject to many other costs:

  • Student Visa: £363 for your UK student visa which will allow you to live and study in the UK throughout your course. Many students are informed of a successful application with less than 3 months to organise their living plans and their visas, this means that the visa has an additional £500 charge to be priority and if it is very short notice, an additonal fee of £1290 must be paid for superior priority. Although all successful international applicants submit their applications over 8 months before their study, they often have to pay these additional fees due to the late decisions of the school they applied to.
  • NHS Surcharge: £470 for the Immigration Health Surcharge per year which will give you access to the UK's National Health Service - this is the discounted student visa holders, so over the course of the PhD this is £1880.
  • Fees for other healthcare: £2000+ on average for dental care, opticians and other services that are not covered by the Immigration Health Surcharge. Many students require visits to dentists, opticians, physiotherapists over the course of their 4 year degree and they have to pay for these out of the £236 they have remaining at the end of the month. If a student needs surgery, the need to pay for this out of pocket; the average cost of a surgery is £2500 in the UK and this results in many students taking out loans to cover the cost.
  • Visiting Home: £200+ for flights to visit home in between your PhD study. Being a postgraduate researcher in a new country can be an isolating experience, but for many PhD students visits home are very infrequent due to the low stipend they receive. Just visiting loved ones twice a year can cost over £1000 for the course of the PhD.

Surcharge & Visa Increase 2024

The government has recently announced that they will be increasing the Immigration Health Surcharge to £1035 per person and visa costs as well this following year:

On 13 July 2023, the UK Government announced that the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) will increase by 66%, from £624 to £1,035 per person per year for most main applicants. UK visa application fees are also set to increase by at least 15%. The Government has not yet announced when this substantial increase in the cost of applying for a UK visa will come into effect. However, the timing of the announcement suggests that it may be as soon as the Autumn. - Richmond Chambers.

Students with Dependents

In the survery conducted, it was discovered that 41% of students with dependents had seriously considered withdrawing from their studies. Out of these students, 83% of these students stated that they were considering withdrawing due to their financial situation.

The average rent for students without dependents for PhD students studying at the University of Bristol is £631 per month; however, for students with dependents, the average monthly rent increases to £860.

Bills also increase for students with dependents, with an average monthly cost of £200 for bills compared to £98 for the average student without dependents. Therefore, on average, a student with dependents spends over 80% of their monthly stipend paying only their rent and bills.
